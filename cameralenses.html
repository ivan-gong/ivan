My Cameras & Lenses Reviews

Using the best camera in the world won't make you the best photographer in the world. Your mind is more important than your cameras and lenses if you want to take beautiful photos. However, cameras & lenses also set the standard of your photography, and they are also important factors of get a good shot. Therefore, here are some reviews of several cameras and lenses that I used.

My Cameras

Nikon D4S

Source: Nikon

Nikon D4S was released in 2014 as Nikon's new flagship professional DSLR camera. This camera has an MSRP of $6500, which makes it become many photographers' dream camera back in 2014. However, I bought this camera after 8 years of its release in 2022. I bought this camera from Japan on eBay for $1300 including tax. Nikon D4S has a 16.1MP full-frame sensor, and it can shoot 11 frames per second continously.

Pros:

  • Outstanding reliability
  • Tough camera body
  • Built-in vertical grip
  • Good low-light capability (high ISO, low noise)
  • Full-frame sensor
  • Accurate colors straight out of camera
  • Accurate and fast auto focus
  • High quality optical viewfinder
  • Long battery durability
  • Safe storage (double card)
  • Cheap price nowadays

Cons:

  • Heavy and large camera body
  • Expensive memory card format (XQD card)
  • Expensive battery
  • Limited video recording time
  • Out-dated design in today's standard

My Lenses

Nikon Nikkor 70-200mm f/2.8G ED VR II

Source: B&H Photo

Nikon Nikkor 70-200mm f/2.8G ED VR II was one of the best Nikon telephoto zoom lenses, which deliver accurate, sharp, and artistic images. The f2.8 maximum aperture gives this lens beautiful brokeh and amazing low-light capability. The MSRP of this lens is $2099. Mounting this lens on a $6500 Nikon D4S then you will have a $8600 camera & lens combo. You just can't take a bad photo with a camera and lens like this.

Pros:

  • Fast & Accurate auto focus
  • Large aperturn
  • High quality build
  • Have image stabilization
  • Creates beautiful brokeh, separate subject from the background clearly

Cons:

  • Heavy, especially when you use it on a professional DSLR
  • Doesn't have manual aperture ring, cannot use on old manual cameras
  • Expensive, only for pros
